Building from the Base

Just like a building, the foundations of our body are the most important part of any physical activity. In the case of our bodies, the foundations are centered around the two girdles - the pelvic girdle and the shoulder girdle. These areas are complex intersections where muscles, bones, and ligaments meet in a hundred different places. Strength and mobility in these regions is paramount to the physical safety of our bodies. Many times, knee pain during running has its roots in our hips, and wrist discomfort can be traced back to misalignment and weakness in the shoulders.

Core Strength

What we think of as our "abs" is actually three layers of powerful muscles, and it is not enough to just exercise the ones that are visible on the outside. Lower back pain is one of the most common physical symptoms of a sedentary lifestyle. The muslces that keep our spine upright are frequently underutilized when we sit for long periods of time. In fact, it only takes 20 minutes of inactivity for our body to start deactivating our stabilizer muscles. Core engagement is something which spans every yoga pose, helping us tune into our bodies and keep our backs safe.

Shoulder Stability

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in the body. As such, there are countless ways you can over-stretch this joint and sprain or dislocate it. The practice of yoga not only opens the shoulders to make them flexible, but strengthens them to make the flexibility safe. Poses like cat & cow, planks, chaturunga, and downward dog help us tune into this important part of our bodies. From yoga, we can learn how to stack our body, engage our muscles, and roll our shoulders in a way that ensures our muscles are engaged in protecting the joint.
